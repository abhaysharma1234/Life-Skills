#### Question 1: What is the Feynman Technique?  
The Feynman Technique is a learning method where we simplify and explain a concept in our own words as if we are teaching someone else about the topic.

---

#### Question 2: What was the most interesting story or idea for you?  
The most compelling idea for me was how our brain benefits from switching between intense, focused (active) thinking and a relaxed, diffused state, allowing us to solve problems creatively.

---

#### Question 3: What are active and diffused modes of thinking?  
- Active thinking: Involves full concentration and focus on solving a problem or learning a concept.  
- Diffused thinking: A relaxed, creative process where the brain explores various ideas and approaches, which often makes innovative solutions.

---

#### Question 4: What are the steps to take when approaching a new topic?  
1. Break the topic into smaller parts.  
2. Identify areas of confusion and clarify them.  
3. Eliminate distractions that slows the  learning process.  
4. Commit to 20 hours of focused practice.

---

#### Question 5: What actions can you take to improve your learning process?  
To improve the learning process, start by explaining concepts simply to myself or others. Use both focused and creative thinking modes, break large topics into smaller sections, and practice consistently. Minimize distractions, and learn from mistakes.
